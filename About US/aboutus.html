About District

The new Tenkasi district was bifurcated from Tirunelveli in 2019. Tenkasi District is located in the south western region of Tamilnadu. It is surrounded by Tirunelveli district in the south east and Virudhunagar in the North. The total geographical area of the district is 2882.44sq.kms. It comprises of 8 Talukas 5 Municipalities, 18 Town panchayaths, 246 revenue villages, 221 village panchayaths.

The Chittar river flows through the district. The famous Coutrallam Falls is on Chittar River. The waterfalls of Courtallam are said to have medicinal value as they run through forest and herbs before their descent. Courtallam is called as “Spa of South India”. Tamil poet ThirikoodaRasappaKavirayar sang about the beauty of this place in his “KutraalaKuravanji”.

The Kutralanathaswamy temple is the location of ChitraSabha, one of the five abodes or Sabhas of Nataraja, a form of Lord Shiva. The temple has inscriptions dating back to the thirteenth century CE. Records show that VeeraPandyan, ParakramaPandyan and the Nayaks ruled the region.

FORMATION OF DISTRICT INDUSTRIES CENTRE, TENKASI

Tenkasi district was formed on 21.12.2019 vide G.O.(ms).No.476 dated 21.12.2019 of Revenue and Disaster Management Development, Revenue Administration wing [RAI(1)] section by bifurcating from Tirunelveli District. Tenkasi is it's headquartering which is the largest Municipality in the area of Tenkasi District.

District Industries Centre, Tenkasi was formed vide G.O.Ms.No.7 MSME (F) Dept dated 29.01.2021 and has been functioning from 26.02.2021

Connectivity To Reach
Roadways

Tenkasi district has a well-connected road network with national and state highways.

Railways

Tenkasi district has rail transport connectivity with several major cities through its railway stations.

Airways

Tenkasi has airway support near Madurai, Thoothukudi and Trivandrum.
